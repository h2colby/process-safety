# PSM COMPLIANCE — CORE REGULATORY KNOWLEDGE

## 1. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK SUMMARY

**OSHA PSM (29 CFR 1910.119):** 14 elements governing management of highly hazardous chemicals. Applies to processes with Appendix A chemicals at/above threshold quantity (TQ), or Category 1 flammable gas / flammable liquid (flash point <100 deg F) at 10,000 lbs or more.

**EPA RMP (40 CFR Part 68):** Risk Management Program for stationary sources with regulated substances above thresholds. Three program levels based on process complexity and accident history.

---

## 2. THE 14 PSM ELEMENTS — COMPACT REFERENCE

### Element 01 — Employee Participation [1910.119(c)]

- **Core requirement:** Written plan of action for employee participation. Consult with employees on PHA conduct and other PSM elements. Provide employee access to PHAs and all PSM information.
- **Records:** Written plan, participation logs, access records
- **Frequency:** Ongoing; review plan annually
- **Common finding:** No written plan exists; plan exists but no evidence of actual consultation
- **Dependencies:** Cross-cutting — feeds into all elements

### Element 02 — Process Safety Information [1910.119(d)]

- **Core requirement:** Compile BEFORE conducting PHA. Three categories:
  - Chemical hazards: toxicity, PEL, physical data, reactivity, corrosivity, stability, mixing hazards
  - Technology: flow diagram, chemistry, max inventory, safe limits, deviation consequences
  - Equipment: materials, P&IDs, electrical classification, relief design/basis, ventilation, design codes, material/energy balances, safety systems
- Document RAGAGEP compliance for equipment. For legacy equipment not built to current codes: document safe design basis.
- **Records:** PSI package per covered process, SDS compilation, P&IDs, design documentation
- **Frequency:** Update when MOC triggers PSI change
- **Common finding:** PSI package incomplete — usually missing P&IDs, relief basis, or operating limits
- **Dependencies:** MUST complete before PHA

### Element 03 — Process Hazard Analysis [1910.119(e)]

- **Core requirement:** Initial PHA on all covered processes using acceptable methodology (What-If, Checklist, What-If/Checklist, HAZOP, FMEA, Fault Tree, or equivalent). Must address: process hazards, previous incidents, engineering/admin controls, consequences of control failure, facility siting, human factors, qualitative health effects. Team requires: engineering expertise + process operations expertise + process-specific experience + PHA methodology knowledge. System to promptly address and resolve findings/recommendations; document actions; communicate to affected employees. Revalidate at least every 5 years. Retain PHAs for life of process.
- **Records:** PHA study reports, team roster, action item register, revalidation reports
- **Frequency:** Initial before startup; revalidation every 5 years
- **Common finding:** Recommendations not resolved/tracked; revalidation overdue; team missing required expertise
- **Dependencies:** Requires completed PSI; feeds into procedures, MI, PSSR

### Element 04 — Operating Procedures [1910.119(f)]

- **Core requirement:** Written procedures for each operating phase: initial startup, normal ops, temporary ops, emergency shutdown, emergency ops, normal shutdown, startup after turnaround/emergency. Operating limits with consequences of deviation and corrective steps. Safety/health info: chemical properties/hazards, exposure prevention, contact response, QC for raw materials, special hazards. Safety systems and their functions. Procedures readily accessible to employees. Annual certification of currency and accuracy. Safe work practices: LOTO, confined space, line opening, facility entry control.
- **Records:** Written procedures, annual certification records, accessibility verification
- **Frequency:** Annual certification; update via MOC
- **Common finding:** Procedures exist but not certified annually; don't cover all operating phases; not accessible at point of use
- **Dependencies:** Informed by PHA findings; feeds into training

### Element 05 — Training [1910.119(g)]

- **Core requirement:** Initial training: process overview + operating procedures. Alternative for existing employees: written certification of knowledge/skills/abilities. Refresher training at least every 3 years; frequency determined in consultation with employees. Documentation: employee identity, date, means used to verify understanding.
- **Records:** Training records (TE-FRM-005), refresher schedule, certifications, training matrix
- **Frequency:** Initial before assignment; refresher every 3 years
- **Common finding:** No means of verification documented; refresher schedule not maintained; new hires not trained before assignment
- **Dependencies:** Requires operating procedures to exist; procedures are what's trained on

### Element 06 — Contractors [1910.119(h)]

- **Core requirement:** Employer responsibilities: evaluate contractor safety performance, inform contractors of hazards/emergency plan, develop safe work practices, periodically evaluate contractor safety. Contractor responsibilities: train employees in safe work practices, document training, ensure employees follow facility safety rules, advise employer of hazards discovered.
- **Records:** Contractor qualification records, training documentation, safety evaluations, injury/illness logs
- **Frequency:** Evaluate before contract award; periodic evaluation during work
- **Common finding:** No evidence of contractor safety evaluation; contractor employees not trained on process hazards; no periodic evaluation
- **Dependencies:** Requires operating procedures (safe work practices)

### Element 07 — Pre-Startup Safety Review [1910.119(i)]

- **Core requirement:** PSSR required for new facilities and modified facilities when MOC is required. Confirm: construction/equipment per design specs, safety/operating/maintenance/emergency procedures in place, PHA performed and recommendations resolved, training completed for affected employees.
- **Records:** PSSR checklists (TE-FRM-002), sign-off documentation
- **Frequency:** Event-driven (new facility or MOC-triggered modification)
- **Common finding:** PSSR not performed for modifications; checklist incomplete; training not verified before startup
- **Dependencies:** Requires PHA complete; requires procedures; requires training; triggered by MOC

### Element 08 — Mechanical Integrity [1910.119(j)]

- **Core requirement:** Applies to: pressure vessels/tanks, piping (including components), relief/vent systems, emergency shutdown systems, controls (including monitoring devices, sensors, alarms, interlocks), pumps. Written procedures for ongoing integrity. Train maintenance employees on process overview and procedures. Inspections and tests per RAGAGEP; frequency per manufacturer recommendations and engineering practices. Document each inspection/test: date, inspector, equipment ID, description, results. Correct deficiencies outside acceptable limits before further use. QA for new construction: suitable fabrication, proper installation, suitable maintenance materials/spare parts.
- **Records:** MI procedures, training records, inspection/test records (TE-FRM-009), deficiency corrections, QA records
- **Frequency:** Per RAGAGEP and manufacturer recommendations
- **Common finding:** No written MI procedures; inspection records incomplete; deficiency correction not documented; no QA program
- **Dependencies:** Informed by PHA (identifies safeguards needing MI)

### Element 09 — Hot Work Permit [1910.119(k)]

- **Core requirement:** Issue hot work permit for operations on or near a covered process. Document fire prevention/protection per 1910.252(a). Permit includes: date(s) authorized, object of hot work, fire prevention requirements met. Keep permit on file until work complete.
- **Records:** Hot work permits (TE-FRM-003)
- **Frequency:** Per occurrence
- **Common finding:** No permit program; permits incomplete; fire watch not documented
- **Dependencies:** Requires operating procedures (safe work practices)

### Element 10 — Management of Change [1910.119(l)]

- **Core requirement:** Written procedures for changes (except replacement in kind) to chemicals, technology, equipment, procedures, and facilities. Pre-change evaluation: technical basis, safety/health impact, procedure modifications, time period, authorization. Inform and train affected employees/contractors before startup. Update PSI and operating procedures/practices as required.
- **Records:** MOC forms (TE-FRM-001), impact evaluations, training records, updated documents
- **Frequency:** Event-driven (each change)
- **Common finding:** No written MOC procedure; changes made without MOC evaluation; PSI/procedures not updated after change; training not completed before startup
- **Dependencies:** Triggers PSI updates, procedure updates, training, PSSR

### Element 11 — Incident Investigation [1910.119(m)]

- **Core requirement:** Investigate incidents that resulted in or could reasonably have resulted in a catastrophic release. Initiate within 48 hours. Team: at least one person knowledgeable in process, contract employee if contractor involved, others with appropriate knowledge. Report minimum: date of incident, date investigation began, description, contributing factors, recommendations. System to promptly address and resolve recommendations; document resolutions and corrective actions. Review report with affected personnel (including contract employees). Retain reports for 5 years.
- **Records:** Investigation reports (TE-FRM-004), action item tracking, review documentation
- **Frequency:** Event-driven; retain 5 years
- **Common finding:** Near-misses not investigated; investigations delayed beyond 48 hours; recommendations not tracked to closure; reports not shared with affected employees
- **Dependencies:** None (program framework element)

### Element 12 — Emergency Planning and Response [1910.119(n)]

- **Core requirement:** Emergency action plan per 1910.38(a) for entire plant. Include procedures for handling small releases. May also be subject to HAZWOPER (1910.120).
- **Records:** Emergency action plan, drill records (TE-FRM-012), coordination with local responders
- **Frequency:** Review annually; drills per plan schedule
- **Common finding:** No plan exists; plan doesn't address small releases; no evidence of drills; no coordination with local responders
- **Dependencies:** None (can develop in parallel)

### Element 13 — Compliance Audits [1910.119(o)]

- **Core requirement:** Certify compliance every 3 years. At least one team member knowledgeable in the process. Report of findings. Promptly determine and document appropriate response to findings; document that deficiencies corrected. Retain two most recent audit reports.
- **Records:** Audit reports (TE-FRM-013), checklist (TE-FRM-007), corrective action documentation
- **Frequency:** Every 3 years
- **Common finding:** Audit not conducted on schedule; findings not tracked to closure; response to findings not documented; only one audit report retained
- **Dependencies:** Should be last — audits the implemented program

### Element 14 — Trade Secrets [1910.119(p)]

- **Core requirement:** Make all information necessary to comply with PSM available without regard to trade secret status. May require confidentiality agreements (per 29 CFR 1910.1200). Employees and designated representatives have access to trade secret information in PHAs, operating procedures, incident investigations, emergency planning, compliance audits.
- **Records:** Confidentiality agreements, access logs
- **Frequency:** Ongoing
- **Common finding:** Information withheld from PHA team; no confidentiality agreement process; employees denied access to necessary information
- **Dependencies:** None (policy element)

## 5. AUDIT-READY CRITERIA (12 POINTS)

1. **Requirement coverage** — no GAP status in crosswalk
2. **Clause traceability** — all 84 clauses mapped to documents
3. **Ownership** — every procedure has R&R section with assigned roles
4. **Frequency** — all periodic actions have defined cadence
5. **Evidence** — every procedure has Records section with form/register references
6. **Document control** — all documents have header blocks (title, number, revision, date, owner, approver)
7. **Cross-reference integrity** — all internal references resolve to real documents
8. **Gap visibility** — gap register exists and is current
9. **Legacy cleanup** — no stray example-company names or placeholder artifacts
10. **Implementation support** — all referenced forms actually exist
11. **Review quality** — at least one self-review pass completed and documented
12. **Publication readiness** — controlled document structure with minimal manual cleanup needed

## 6. RMP PROGRAM LEVEL LOGIC

**Program 3:** Process subject to OSHA PSM OR SIC code in [2611-2899, 3011-3099, 2911, 4612, 4613, 4619, 5171]

**Program 1:** No offsite-consequence release in 5 years AND worst-case analysis shows no public receptor impact AND emergency response coordinated with local responders

**Program 2:** Everything else

Most plugin users will be Program 3 (PSM-covered = Program 3 automatically).

## 7. ERROR HANDLING RULES

- **WebFetch failure:** Mark data as `REQUIRES MANUAL ENTRY -- data source unavailable`. Never block progress.
- **Chemical not in lists:** Warn but do not assert non-regulation. Flammable catch-all may still apply.
- **Missing company data:** Use `REQUIRES COMPANY INPUT` placeholder. Never invent facility facts.
- **Conflicting information:** Document the conflict, propose resolution, ask user to decide.
- **State file missing:** Rebuild from existing documents if `PSM_PROGRAM/` directory exists. Otherwise start fresh.

---

## 8. COMMON STARTUP SITUATIONS

- **1-5 person company:** CEO is PSM Program Manager + PSM Coordinator. Lead engineer is Operations + Maintenance Manager. Everyone gets trained on everything.
- **Pre-revenue / pre-construction:** Generate program framework now. Many items will be `NEEDS COMPANY INPUT` until facility is built. PSI and PHA required before startup.
- **Pilot plant vs. production:** Both may be covered if TQs are met. Separate covered process entries.
- **Contract manufacturers:** The contract manufacturer may need their own PSM program. The hiring company has contractor obligations under Element 06.

## 9. EPA RMP (40 CFR PART 68) — COMPACT REFERENCE

### Applicability
Stationary sources with regulated substances (toxic or flammable) above threshold quantities per 40 CFR 68.130. Most PSM-covered facilities are also RMP-covered (Program 3).

### Program Levels
- **Program 3:** Process subject to OSHA PSM OR NAICS code in [32211, 32411, 32511, 325181, 325188, 325192, 325199, 325211, 325311, 32532]
- **Program 1:** No offsite-consequence release in 5 years AND worst-case shows no public receptor impact AND emergency response coordinated with locals
- **Program 2:** Everything else

### RMP Submission Sections (68.150-68.185)
1. Executive Summary (68.155) — narrative overview of facility and prevention program
2. Registration (68.160) — ~25 data fields: facility info, contacts, NAICS, chemicals, quantities
3. Worst-Case Scenarios (68.165) — per-process OCA results for most severe release
4. Alternative Scenarios (68.170) — per-substance realistic release scenario OCA results
5. 5-Year Accident History (68.42) — per-incident structured data
6. Prevention Program (68.65-68.89) — mirrors OSHA PSM 14 elements
7. Emergency Response (68.90-68.95) — response plan, exercises, coordination
8. Certification (68.185) — owner/operator certification statement

### OCA Methodology (EPA Simplified Method)
- **Toxic worst-case:** Release rate = total qty / 10 min (gas); pool evaporation formula (liquid). Distance via EPA lookup tables or Gaussian dispersion. Parameters: 1.5 m/s wind, F stability, ground level, highest temp in 3 years.
- **Flammable worst-case:** TNT-equivalent model (10% yield factor), distance to 1 psi overpressure: d = 55.7 × W_TNT^(1/3) feet. Also calc BLEVE fireball for liquefied gases: d = 5.25 × mass^0.397 feet.
- **Alternative scenarios:** Realistic parameters (3 m/s wind, D stability), can credit active mitigation (water sprays 55% reduction, scrubbers 90% reduction).
- **Dual-hazard chemicals:** Run BOTH toxic and flammable OCA. Report both distances.
